This dataset provides detailed information about employees across different departments, demographics, job roles, and performance-related attributes
1. Workforce Overview

The dataset contains a total number of employees along with their demographic, job-related, and satisfaction-related details. It includes fields such as department, age band, gender, job role, education, monthly income, experience, and attrition status.

2. Attrition Overview

Attrition indicates employees who have left the organization.

The dataset shows that a portion of employees have attrited (“Yes” in the Attrition column).

The calculated attrition rate reflects the proportion of employees who left compared to the total dataset size.

This helps identify potential areas of concern, such as roles or departments with high turnover.

3. Department Distribution

Employees belong primarily to three departments:

Research & Development (R&D) – typically the largest group.

Sales – second largest.

Human Resources (HR) – smallest department.

The distribution gives insight into which areas of the business hold the most workforce.

4. Gender Distribution

The dataset includes both male and female employees.

A higher number of employees fall into one gender category depending on the department or job role.

This helps analyze gender diversity across the company.

7. Key Observations

The organization employs more people in R&D, which aligns with product-based or innovation-driven companies.

Sales holds the second largest workforce, essential for business growth.

Attrition exists in all age groups but may be higher in certain bands or job roles depending on workload and career progression.

The workforce appears diverse in age and job responsibilities, offering opportunities for targeted HR policies.
